Victoria is a state in Australia. It is the second smallest state with a land area of 227,444 square kilometers, the second most populous state with a population of over 6.5 million, and the most densely populated state in Australia. The state is home to 20 of Australia's largest Four of the cities: Melbourne, Geelong, Ballarat and Bendigo. The population is culturally diverse and 35.1% of residents are immigrants.

This visualization will focus primarily on house prices in 2016 and 2017. Analyze which factors affect house prices rising and which cause house prices fall.

How Suburb Affect House Price

The dataset is created from melb_data.csv. The visualization shows the average house price per suburb. The bar chart on the left shows which suburb's data has more data, so it has a higher confidence.
From map visualisation shows that Boronia have the highest average house price with $90054/m^2. Suburbs in the middle of the map generally have higher house prices, and suburbs farther from the center have lower house prices.

How the number of Rooms Affect House Price

As the visualization shows, and not as expected, houses with more rooms tend to have higher prices.

Homeowner, house with 5, 6, and 7 rooms have higher prices than those with 8, 9, and 10 rooms. Houses with more than five rooms will not be affected by the number of rooms.
